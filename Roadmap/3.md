# Evolution, Systems Upgrades and Quantum Coherence and Decoherence

The signature property of [quantum matter waves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matter_wave), [wave interference](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wave_interference), relies on coherence. [Quantum coherence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coherence_(physics)#Quantum_coherence) is a property of quantum systems that allows them to maintain a phase relationship between states. [Quantum decoherence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_decoherence) is the process by which this coherence is lost due to interactions with the environment. Although researchers are working on ways to get instrumnets to minimize decoherence, such as improved quantum sensor isolation and error correction techniques, we really want to better understand everything we can about these properties so that we can devise systems that might be tunable, capable of somehow adapting or reconfiguring themselves, adjusting and tolerating faults, correcting weaknesses, evolving and continuously working better and better over almost infinite [compared to human scale] lifespans. Obviously, our understanding will never be complete and we need to think of systems that allow for learning, but trying to contemplate and understand portions of [quantum electrodynamics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_electrodynamics) better helps us to overcome the brittleness or breakability in our designs ... and, *opens the door*, so to speak, for continual upgrading through different sorts of learning, adapting or evolutionary processes. 

We need to begin by recognizing and exploring our limits, knowledgewise ... human intuition or basic common sense is largely incapable of really pushing into these realms, because human experience is not at the [particle physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Particle_physics) level. Most of us just don't ordinarily spend many of our waking moments with direct experience troubleshooting and making stuff work in these realms, ie it takes rather advanced levels of experience to even begin understand how we might begin to think practically about this material. Toward that end, we want to delve deeper, beyond the questions below, to really begin exploring and testing our understanding experientially, eg tweaking/experimenting with models in papers with code/data, especially in the topics like [Quantum Coherence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coherence_(physics)#Quantum_coherence) and [Quantum Decoherence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_decoherence) ... but there are plenty of other tangential areas that also are necessary to cover in sufficient depth include:

6. **What new theories could emerge from comparing quantum tunneling in photosynthesis with electron transport in semiconductor-based photovoltaics?** Photosynthetic organisms utilize [dynamic and complex networks of pigments bound within light-harvesting complexes to transfer solar energy from antenna complexes to reaction centers](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10375525/#sec3). Understanding the principles underlying the efficiency of these energy transfer processes, and how they may be incorporated into artificial light-harvesting systems, is facilitated by the construction of easily tunable model systems ... for example, a protein-based model can be divised to mimic directional energy transfer between light-harvesting complexes using a circular permutant of the tobacco mosaic virus coat protein (cpTMV), which self-assembles into a 34-monomer hollow disk. This model ultimately provides a controllable and modifiable platform for understanding photosynthetic interassembly energy transfer and, through its study, may lead to the design of more efficient functional light-harvesting materials. Photosynthesis naturally boasts remarkable efficiency in energy transfer, often attributed to [quantum coherence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coherence_(physics)#Quantum_coherence) ... with differences [or why some photosynthetic processes are less efficient in some conditions] usually attributed to [quantum decoherence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_decoherence). The energetic landscape in commonly studied bacterial light harvesting tends to have a simple dissipative organization with chromophores nearer the reaction center having lower transition energies. As such, incoherent hopping provides a reasonable mechanism for navigating this landscape while coherent transport simplifies the process by simply course-graining the landscape. Conversely, for higher plants, the landscape is rugged and not obviously “tilted” toward the reaction center. Coherent transport is likely to be much more important in a higher plant environment. Thus, organism-mediated [regulation of coherence ***really matters*** in photosynthesis](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1876619611000684). Photosynthetic systems utilize specific molecular arrangements and protein scaffolds to facilitate electron tunneling -- analyzing and experimenting with these structures could inspire the development of novel materials with tailored electronic or logic properties. Photosynthesis involves both quantum mechanical processes (tunneling) and classical ones (electron transport) -- hybrid models which accurately capture these intertwined mechanisms should provide energy conversion insights. Vibrational modes within molecules play a crucial role in facilitating and controlling electron transfer in photosynthesis. The complexity of quantum tunneling in photosynthesis necessitates advanced computational methods, but just the process of attempting to apply better quantum computing techniques to simulate these processes might provide new testable hypotheses for insights into the underlying mechanisms.
